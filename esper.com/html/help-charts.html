Charts and Analysis

Calendar Hours

This bar chart shows the total amount of hours you've allocated to each of your calendars. If you have separate work and personal calendars, you can use this chart to compare how much time you're allocating between your professional and personal goals.

Each segment in the bar chart represents an event. Hover over or click them for more details on that event.

Calendar Percentages

This stacked bar chart shows the relative percentage of time between each of your calendars. If you have separate work and personal calendars, you can use this chart to compare how much time you're allocating between your professional and personal goals.

Click on a bar segment to see the list of events that count towards that segment.

Calendar Pie Chart

This pie chart shows relative percentages you've allocated to each of your calendars. This works just like the "Calendar Percentages" stacked bar chart, except it's a pie.

Event Durations

This histogram groups your events by how long they take. You can use this chart to spot unusually long events or to check whether your time is being taken up by a few big events or lots of little things.

Each segment in the bar chart represents an event. Hover over or click them for more details on that event.

Guest Groups Pie Chart

This pie chart shows which groups or organizations (as determined by e-mail address) are the most common among guests invited to events on your calendar. You can use this chart to make sure you're spending time with the right people.

Click on a segment of the pie to drill down into how much time you spend with each person in that group.

Label Hours

This bar chart shows the total number of hours allocated to your own custom categories over fixed intervals of time. You can use this to figure out whether you're on track to hit your goals.

Each segment in the bar chart represents an event. Hover over or click them for more details on that event.

Label Percentages

This stacked bar chart shows the relative allocation of time between each of your different custom categories. You can use this to see if you're spending too much time on the wrong things.

Click on a bar segment to see the list of events that count towards that segment

Label Pie Chart

This stacked bar chart shows the relative allocation of time between each of your different custom categories. This works just like the "Labels Percentages" stacked bar chart, except it's a pie.

Monthly Overview

This calendar grid shows you how many events and how much time you're scheduled for on each day of the month. If you have custom labels defined, it will colorize each day based on which labels apply to that day. You can use this to get a quick heads-up view of how your week or month is headed.

Top Guests

This bar chart lists the people who are most frequently invited to events on your calendar. You can use this chart to make sure you're spending enough time with the right people.

Each segment in the bar chart represents an event. Hover over or click them for more details on that event.

A Note on Percentages

If you have overlapping calendar events, Esper will adjust the percentage of time allocated to each event by dividing the overlapping time evenly among each event. Therefore, a one-hour event may occupy more space on a pie chart than another one-hour event if the latter overlaps another event.

Esper will also divided time evenly between different categories of events depending on the chart. For example, in the Guest Groups Pie Chart, time is divided evenly between each attendee in the meeting.
